Chapter VII · A Letter

For you,
on every May VII.

7 May, 2026

My Aku,

I keep going back to that morning. The light, the quiet, the way the day held its breath before everything began. The 4:34 cab, your lipstick going on in the back seat, the way you looked when you stepped out — I think a part of me will stand in that morning forever.

Do you remember how all of this started? You wanted my height before you wanted my name. You wanted to call instead of text because you needed the voice first. On the second night you told me a partner should love their person a little — and I told you it would be the whole thing, or nothing. We both meant it.

People kept asking how it felt — to get a ring, to sign a paper, to become a wife. I didn't know how to answer them. Because nothing about it felt like an event. It felt like a recognition. Like the world finally caught up with something you and I already knew that first November.

Two months in, you told me I was driving you crazy and that you weren't like that — usually. I knew then. Some things you can't un-know. Three months after that, in a country I'd been keeping for us, I was building a small Valentine's page and watching you open it again, and again, and again. Some things stay built.

7 May, 2026 was
the best day of my life.

♥

The days that followed — the slow ones, the ordinary ones, the ones where we burned a tea and forgot which side of the bed we'd chosen — those are the ones I'll remember most. Because they were the first time my life felt like ours.

I don't have grand things to give you. Just my mornings, and my evenings, and the years in between. And this — a small page on a small corner of the internet, where I get to write you down, the way you deserve to be written down.

Wherever we end up, whichever May VII finds us — I'll find you first. I promise.

Forever yours —

Ashish

P.S. The seventh of May is now both of our favourite day. Don't let me forget.
