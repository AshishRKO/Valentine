Chapter VII · A Letter

For you,
on every May VII.

7 May, 2026

My Aku,

I keep going back to that morning. The light, the quiet, the way the day held its breath before everything began. I think a part of me will stand in that morning forever.

People kept asking how it felt — to get a ring, to sign a paper, to become a wife. I didn't know how to answer them. Because nothing about it felt like an event. It felt like a recognition. Like the world finally caught up with something you and I already knew.

The days that followed — the slow ones, the ordinary ones, the ones where we burned a tea and forgot which side of the bed we'd chosen — those are the ones I'll remember most. Because they were the first time my life felt like ours.

I don't have grand things to give you. Just my mornings, and my evenings, and the years in between. And this — a small page on a small corner of the internet, where I get to write you down, the way you deserve to be written down.

Wherever we end up, whichever May VII finds us — I'll find you first. I promise.

Forever yours —

Ashish

P.S. The seventh of May is now both of our favourite day. Don't let me forget.
